Common Birds You are Likely to Find Near You

    • House Crow

    The House Crow is found throughout India, but only near human habitation. It is an omnivore and eats carrion, eggs, young birds,vegetables, fruits and kitchen waste. It usually mobs smaller raptors like the Shikra with one or two birds forcing it to drop the food. It also mobs bigger raptors like the Black Kite too. It has a gray neck and an all black body, beak, and legs.

    Large-billed Crow

    Similar to the House Crow, it is one of the most common birds of India. Besides living in and near human habitation, it is found in forests, jungles, mountains, and other places.It is larger and has a large bill(beak) than the House Crow.Habits are similar to House Crow's.

    Rock Pigeon

    A common kind of pigeon that was domesticated more than 5000 years ago, it was found in the jungle and moved to human habitation. The ones you find in your your area are feral pigeons. The ones that are found in jungles and forests are the originals and are hard to find nowadays. It eats mainly grains and is found feeding in mixed flocks with other birds.

    Asian Koel

    Asian koels are one of the most common birds of the Cuckoo family. Their diet mainly consists of fruits and berries. Only the males can sing. The males are all black with red eyes and a lime-silverish beak. The females are dark brown and spotted with red eyes, lime-silverish beak and light brown on the head. They lay their eggs in crow nests. The female can lay its eggs whenever it wants unlike other birds(except the cuckoo family).

    Common Myna

    The most Common bird of India, it is found in all habitats, even in the mountains. They are very noisy in the breeding season, often flying around and sitting on trees in groups. They are omnivores and eat mainly lizards, insects, grains, fruits and berries. They sometimes mob other birds like pigeons that come near them. They have a black head, brown body,and turmeric-colored eye-patch, bill and legs.

    Black Kite

    A large raptor that is found all over India, it is also one of the most common. It is mainly found near human habitation, soaring in the air. It usually scavenges, but hunts occasionally. It is found in large numbers in open areas and where prey is abundant. It is frequently mobbed by crows. It soars at great heights in midday and when the heat is high. It is a common sight in dumpyards, preying upon the rats and mice.
