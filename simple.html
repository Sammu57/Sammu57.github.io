Top 5 Bird Hotspots of Chennai

  1. Pallikaranai Marsh (பள்ளிக்கரணை சதுப்பு நிலம்)
  2. Guindy National Park
  3. Adayaru Estuary
  4. Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras
  5. Theosophical Society

Pallikaranai Marsh (பள்ளிக்கரணை சதுப்பு நிலம்)

Click here to view the map of Pallikaranai marsh.

Information:

The #1 hotspot of Chennai, Pallikaranai marsh has been home to an astounding 200+ bird species(including migrants) Some highlights include:

  • Greater Spotted Eagle
  • Eastern Yellow Wagtail
  • Lesser Flamingo
  • Streaked Weaver
  • Asian Pied Starling
    • It is very huge, having various ends, such as the Southern end, Sholinganallur end, etc. Being a wetland, it is an ideal habitat for birds. A wetland can be very useful to us in many ways. One is, when there is a flood, the wetlands drain up all that water and store them. The second one is that how much ever dirty the water may be, It filters it and gives only the cleanest of water. But, even though being very useful, Pallikaranai is being eaten up by industries. The vast range that it had once, has shrunk highly, and is shrinking drastically too. Though it is a protected area, industries are not following the rules and are building more and more, letting all the waste into Pallikaranai marsh. If the wetlands had not been destroyed people would not have suffered from floods, from diseases caused by drinking dirty water, and many other things. We have to save them.

    Guindy National Park

    Click here to view the map.

    Information:

    The #2 hotspot of Chennai, it houses around 200 birds(including migrants). Some highlights include:

  • Malayan Night heron
  • Oriental Turtle Dove
  • Dollarbird
  • Orange-breasted Green Pigeon
  • Eurasian Hobby
    • It is full of a great variety of flora and fauna. For animals, some endangered species include the blackbuck, and various snake species, a type of pit viper being the latest. It is a protected area and you will have to have permission to enter. The Guindy Children's park is in the adjacent compound. This place doesn't face many threats since it is protected much more than Pallikaranai, except for occasional poaching and logging.

    Adayaru Estuary

    Click here to view the map of Adayar

    Information:

    Being Chennai's #3 bird hotspot, Adayaru estuary houses an amazing 190+ species(including migrants). Some highlights are:

  • Amur Falcon
  • Lesser Black-backed Gull
  • Daurian Starling
  • Desert Wheatear
  • Gray-fronted Green Pigeon

    • Located near the beach, waders can be seen very easily moving around. The Theosophical Society is very close too. It is not a protected area and one can go there anytime they like. One can sight much sealife on the shores too.

    Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras

    Click here to view the map of the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras.

    Information:

    The #4 hotspot of Chennai, the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras houses more than 190 species of birds(including migrants). Some highlights are:

  • Eastern Yellow Wagtail
  • Brown Boobook
  • Lesser Frigatebird
  • Lesser Cuckoo
  • Ruddy-breasted Crake
    • With a huge campus covered with nature, IIT Madras has got a lot of wildlife inside. Blackbucks, Spotted deer or Chital, snakes such as Rat Sankes and a huge variety of birds, butterflies and other insects make up excellent biodiversity. It is a campus full of life.

    Theosophical Society

    Click here to view the map of the Theosophical Society.

    Information:

    The Theosophical Society, located near the Adayaru Estuary, is the #5 bird hotspot of Chennai, with more than 170 species(including migrants). Some highlights are:

  • Forest Wagtail
  • Brown-rumped Minivet
  • Gray-fronted Green Pigeon
  • Greater Racket-tailed Drongo
  • Black-capped Kingfisher

    • Since it is near the Adayaru Estuary, the Theosophical Society shares a few species like the Gray-fronted Green Pigeon. One might need permission to enter. Also, there are many trees inside, making it an ideal habitat for many birds.
